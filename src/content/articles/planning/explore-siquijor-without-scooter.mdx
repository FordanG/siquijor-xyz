Every Siquijor guide assumes you will rent a scooter. Every forum thread asks about motorcycle rental. Every blog post details scenic driving routes. But what if you cannot or do not want to ride a scooter? What if you arrived without a license, are traveling with young children, are simply nervous about driving on unfamiliar roads, or simply prefer not to?

The good news is that Siquijor is fully explorable without two wheels. This guide covers every option for non-drivers, from hiring tricycle drivers by the day to joining organized tours, walking between nearby attractions, and maximizing your time in walkable areas.

## Why Skip the Scooter

There are legitimate reasons to explore Siquijor without a motorcycle:

**Licensing and Confidence:** International driving permits vary in recognition, and some travelers simply do not feel confident navigating narrow mountain roads with occasional potholes and oncoming traffic. Siquijor's roads, while improved, still challenge inexperienced riders.

**Family Travel:** When traveling with infants, elderly relatives, or anyone who cannot ride as a passenger safely, the tricycle or multi-cab becomes the default transport.

**Physical Limitations:** Some travelers have conditions that preclude motorcycle riding, whether temporary or permanent. Siquijor should not be off-limits for them.

**Cost at Scale:** A family of four renting one scooter is awkward and potentially dangerous. One tricycle or multi-cab handles everyone comfortably.

**Enjoying the View:** Riding a scooter requires constant attention to the road. Traveling by tricycle means you can look around, take photos, and actually enjoy the scenery without risking your life at a hairpin turn.

## Tricycle Tours: Your Best Option

The tricycle is Siquijor's workhorse public vehicle. Modified motorcycle rigs with a covered passenger carriage in the back, tricycles serve as the island's primary form of public transport and the key to exploring without your own wheels.

### How Tricycle Hiring Works

**Charter Rate (Full Day):** PHP 1,500 to PHP 2,500 for a full day (8 to 10 hours), depending on negotiations and season. This covers one driver and the full coastal tour or a designated area.

**Charter Rate (Half Day):** PHP 800 to PHP 1,500 for 4 to 5 hours, suitable for focused area exploration.

**Per Trip:** Short hops between nearby attractions typically cost PHP 30 to PHP 80 per trip, similar to taxi fares.

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Always negotiate the full rate upfront before committing
- Ask what the rate includes (waiting time, number of stops, area coverage)
- Peak season (January to April, Holy Week) rates trend higher
- Booking through your accommodation often secures better rates than street hiring
- Multi-day charters can be negotiated at PHP 1,200 to PHP 1,800 per day

### Full Coastal Tricycle Tour

The standard day tour covers the island's main attractions:

**Typical Route:**
- Paliton Beach (sunrise or sunset)
- Old Enchanted Balete Tree (fish spa)
- San Juan area beaches
- Salagdoong Beach (cliff jumping platforms)
- Cambugahay Falls (main waterfall)
- Lazi Church and Convent
- Return to base

**What to Expect:**
- Driver waits at each stop while you explore
- Comfortable covered seating for 4 to 6 passengers
- Air conditioning not available but open-sided design catches island breeze
- Stops are flexible within reason

**Cost Benefit:** At PHP 1,500 to PHP 2,000 for a full day shared among 4 passengers, the per-person cost (PHP 375 to PHP 500) is comparable to individual scooter rental plus fuel.



### Area-Specific Tricycle Routes

Not every trip requires the full island loop. Breaking your exploration into themed tours is more efficient and less exhausting.

**North Coast Focus (San Juan to Larena):**
- Paliton Beach
- Old Enchanted Balete Tree
- Salagdoong Beach
- Northern municipality exploration
- Duration: Half day to full day depending on stops

**East and South Coast Focus (Lazi and Siquijor Town):**
- Cambugahay Falls
- Lazi Church and Convent
- Mount Bandilaan Butterfly Garden
- Southern beaches
- Duration: Half day

**Interior Focus:**
- Cantabon Cave
- Mountain barangays
- Interior waterfalls (requires guide coordination)
- Duration: Full day recommended

## Public Tricycle Routes

Tricycles also operate on fixed routes between municipalities, functioning as a more affordable public bus system.

**How Public Tricycles Work:**
- Tricycles run between towns throughout the day
- You wait at the main road and flag one down
- Passengers share until the vehicle is full
- Fares are fixed per leg (approximately PHP 20 to PHP 50 between municipalities)

**Limitations:**
- Fixed routes may not take you directly to attractions
- Schedules are unpredictable
- Waiting time for enough passengers can be significant
- Less flexible than chartering

**Best For:** Short hops between towns when you are not in a hurry and want maximum local authenticity.

## Multi-cab Rentals for Groups

For larger groups or families, the multi-cab (van or minibus) becomes an option.

**When to Consider Multi-cab:**
- Groups of 8 or more travelers
- Family reunions or organized tours
- Travelers requiring air conditioning
- Corporate retreats or team activities

**Availability and Cost:**
- Multi-cabs are less common than tricycles
- Arrange through tour operators rather than street hiring
- Full-day rates range from PHP 4,000 to PHP 8,000 depending on vehicle and negotiation

## Walking and Hiking Within Towns

Siquijor's compact tourist zones are walkable without any vehicle at all.

### Walkable San Juan

San Juan, the main tourist hub, is remarkably manageable on foot:

**Attractions Within Walking Distance:**
- Beach areas and Paliton Beach (20 to 30 minutes on foot from central San Juan)
- Tubod Marine Sanctuary (15 to 20 minutes)
- Restaurants, bars, and cafes along the main road
- Small convenience stores and sari-sari shops

**Walking Tips:**
- Use the main coastal road rather than shortcuts
- Wear sun protection and bring water
- Accept that some walks will take 20 to 30 minutes each way
- Combine walking days with one tricycle day for remote attractions



### Day Hiking Options

Siquijor's interior offers several hiking opportunities that require no vehicle at all:

**Mount Bandilaan Trails:**
- Multiple trail options from easy walks to challenging hikes
- Views across the island and to neighboring islands on clear days
- Mount Bandilaan National Park base is accessible by tricycle

**Coastal Walks:**
- Several stretches of beach are walkable at low tide
- Connecting beach coves between San Juan and Lazi
- Sandbars appear during certain tidal conditions

## Organized Day Tours

For travelers who prefer not to arrange their own transport, organized tours offer a structured alternative.

**What Organized Tours Include:**
- Transport from your accommodation and back
- All entrance fees
- Guide narration and context
- Lunch or specified meals depending on package

**Typical Tour Cost:**
- Full day island tour: PHP 2,500 to PHP 4,000 per person (group joining)
- Private tour: PHP 6,000 to PHP 10,000 total (can split among group)
- Includes logistics, planning, and execution

**Where to Book:**
- Your accommodation (most offer tour arrangements)
- Local tour operators in San Juan
- Facebook groups for Siquijor travelers
- Advance booking recommended during peak season

**Tour Advantages:**
- No negotiation stress
- Someone else handles all logistics
- Guide provides context and local knowledge
- Guaranteed departure (vs. waiting for tricycle availability)

## Multi-day Exploration Strategy

If you have more than two or three days on the island, combining transport methods works well.

### Recommended Approach

**Day 1: San Juan Base**
- Walk to nearby beaches
- Tricycle afternoon for Paliton Beach and Balete Tree
- Evening at local restaurants

**Day 2: Tricycle Full Island Loop**
- Hire tricycle for entire day
- Cover all major attractions
- Cover maximum ground

**Day 3: Focused Area Tour**
- Another tricycle half-day for specific interest (waterfalls, south coast, etc.)
- Or organized dive or snorkeling tour with their own transport

**Day 4: Rest and Walk**
- Relax in San Juan
- Walk to beach areas
- Enjoy slower pace without transport logistics

### Accommodation-Based Strategy

Where you stay significantly affects your transport needs:

**San Juan:** Most walkable base with everything in relative proximity. Tricycles are readily available for day trips to remote areas.

**Siquijor Town:** More central but less tourist infrastructure. Expect to arrange tricycle transport for most excursions.

**Lazi:** Quieter base with good access to Cambugahay Falls and southern attractions. Tricycles less frequent but available through accommodation.

**Remote Areas:** Staying far from town centers essentially requires your own transport or full-day tricycle charter. Not recommended for non-drivers unless your accommodation provides transport arrangements.

## Practical Tips for Non-Drivers

### Before You Go

1. **Confirm accommodation can arrange tricycle transport** before booking properties in remote areas
2. **Download offline maps** for reference even if you are not navigating independently
3. **Carry sufficient cash** for tricycle charters and tips
4. **Pack light** if you plan to walk between attractions

### On the Ground

1. **Book tricycles through your accommodation** rather than on the street
2. **Confirm full-day rate includes waiting time** at each stop
3. **Ask about toilet facilities** along your planned route
4. **Carry water and snacks** for long tour days
5. **Be flexible** with timing; schedules on the island are fluid

### Budget Reality

Non-scooter transport costs more per day than self-driving but less than many travelers assume:

| Transport Mode | Daily Cost | Per Person (4-share) |
|----------------|------------|----------------------|
| Scooter rental | PHP 350-450 | PHP 175-225 |
| Tricycle charter | PHP 1,500-2,500 | PHP 375-625 |
| Organized tour | PHP 2,500-4,000 | PHP 2,500-4,000 |

The per-person premium for tricycle transport over scooter rental is PHP 200 to PHP 400 per day. For families or groups, the difference narrows or reverses entirely.

## Common Concerns Addressed

**Will I miss attractions?** No. All major attractions are accessible by tricycle. The only difference is that you sit back and look at the scenery instead of focusing on the road.

**Are tricycle drivers reliable?** Most drivers are professional and trustworthy. Using accommodation referrals rather than random hiring reduces risk.

**Can I hire a tricycle last minute?** In peak season, pre-booking is safer. In low season, finding availability same-day is usually fine.

**Is it awkward being dependent on someone else?** Not really. Tricycle drivers often become informal guides, sharing local knowledge and recommendations you would miss entirely in a scooter.

**What about rainy season?** Tricycle travel is more comfortable than scooter travel in rain. Your driver handles the wet roads, and you stay dry in the covered carriage.

## Final Thoughts

Siquijor deserves to be experienced by every type of traveler, not just those comfortable on motorcycles. Tricycles are not a compromise option. For groups, families, and anyone who prefers not to drive, tricycle charters are often the better choice: you see more because you are not navigating, you travel safer because professionals handle the roads, and you build relationships with local drivers who become your guides and connectors on the island.

The island reveals itself differently when you travel by tricycle. You notice the changes in light across rice paddies, the small shrines at crossroads, the way village life unfolds along the road. These details are harder to see through a helmet visor at 40 kilometers per hour.

Skip the scooter if you prefer. Siquijor will welcome you either way.
