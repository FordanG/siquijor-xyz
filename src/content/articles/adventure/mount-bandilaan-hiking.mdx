Rising 557 meters above the Sulu Sea, **Mount Bandilaan** is Siquijor's highest peak and one of its most rewarding adventures. This protected national park offers more than just stunning 360-degree views—it's the spiritual heart of the island, home to medicinal plants used by traditional healers and the sacred site of the famous Healing Festival.

## Why Visit Mount Bandilaan

### The Views
From the summit area, you can see all of Siquijor spread out below you, with the neighboring islands of Cebu, Negros, and Bohol visible on clear days. It's the only place on the island where you can truly appreciate how small and beautiful Siquijor is.

### The Medicinal Forest
Mount Bandilaan is the source of various medicinal trees and plants used by the mananambals (traditional healers) of Siquijor. The forest here isn't just beautiful—it's a living pharmacy that has sustained traditional healing practices for generations.

### The Cultural Significance
This is sacred ground for Siquijor's healers. During Holy Week, the foot of Mount Bandilaan becomes the venue for the famous **Pahi-uli: Siquijor Healing Festival**, where traditional healers gather to showcase their craft and perform rituals.

### The Adventure
Getting to Mount Bandilaan is an adventure in itself. The steep road, the jungle-lined path, and the sense of ascending above the coastal world below make the journey as memorable as the destination.

<ProTip type="insider">
Passing Mount Bandilaan was my first time driving on a really steep road, and it was a memorable experience. If you're not confident on a scooter, consider going with someone who is—or join a guided tour. You'll understand why once you're climbing!
</ProTip>

## The Drive Up

### Road Conditions
Let's be honest: the road to Mount Bandilaan is challenging. Expect:
- **Steep inclines** — Some sections are quite steep
- **Rough patches** — Not all parts of the road are well-developed
- **Narrow sections** — Limited passing room in some areas
- **No proper railings** — Be cautious near edges
- **Moss in rainy season** — Can make the road slippery

### The Experience
As you ascend, the landscape changes. The coastal coconut palms give way to taller trees, and the air becomes noticeably cooler. You'll pass through dense forest with towering trees on either side, creating a canopy that blocks much of the sunlight.

This atmospheric drive is part of the Mount Bandilaan experience—the sense of leaving the beach-focused coast behind and entering a different, more mystical part of Siquijor.

<Warning severity="caution">
It is NOT advisable to visit the park in late afternoon, especially if you're not used to the place. The tall trees along the way mean light fades quickly, and the lack of proper road railing makes driving in low light dangerous.
</Warning>

## What to See

### The Summit Area
The peak area features viewing platforms where you can take in the 360-degree panorama. On clear days, you can see:
- The entire coastline of Siquijor
- Neighboring Negros Island
- Cebu in the distance
- Bohol on exceptionally clear days

### The Station of the Cross
A devotional path featuring the Stations of the Cross leads through the forested area, reflecting the island's Catholic heritage.

### Flora and Fauna
The protected area is home to:
- Medicinal plants used by healers
- Various bird species
- Butterflies
- Native trees and vegetation

### The Butterfly Sanctuary Nearby
The Siquijor Butterfly Garden is located near Mount Bandilaan, in Cang-apa village. You can combine the two visits easily.

## Best Time to Visit

### Time of Day
- **Early morning (6-10 AM):** Best for clear views, cooler temperatures, and safer driving
- **Midday:** Possible but hotter
- **Late afternoon:** NOT recommended due to quickly fading light

### Season
- **Dry season (November-May):** Best road conditions, clearer views
- **Rainy season:** Road becomes slippery with moss; not recommended

### During Holy Week
If your visit coincides with Holy Week, the Healing Festival at Mount Bandilaan is a unique cultural experience. Traditional healers perform rituals, prepare medicines, and welcome visitors to observe their practices.

<Warning severity="danger">
It is NOT recommended to visit the park during rainy weather. The road becomes dangerous with slippery conditions caused by abundant moss. Wait for dry conditions to make the journey safely.
</Warning>

## Getting There

Mount Bandilaan is located in the central highlands of Siquijor.

**Travel Times (approximate):**
- From San Juan: 40-50 minutes
- From Lazi: 30-40 minutes
- From Larena: 45 minutes
- From Siquijor Town: 40 minutes

**Directions:**
1. Head inland from your coastal starting point
2. Follow signs toward Mount Bandilaan or Cang-apa
3. The road begins to climb significantly as you enter the park area
4. Continue to the summit viewing area

## What to Bring

**Essential:**
- Sturdy footwear (not flip-flops!)
- At least 1 liter of water
- Camera
- Cash (for any small fees or donations)

**Recommended:**
- Sunscreen
- Hat or cap
- Light rain jacket (weather can change)
- Snacks
- Fully charged phone

**For Scooter Riders:**
- Full tank of gas
- Confidence on steep roads
- Patience for slow climbing

<ProTip>
Fill up your scooter before heading up. There are no gas stations in the mountains, and the climbing uses more fuel than flat coastal riding. The last thing you want is to run out of gas on a steep road!
</ProTip>

## Combining with Other Attractions

Mount Bandilaan can be combined with several nearby attractions:

**Option 1: Mountain Day**
1. **8:00 AM** — Mount Bandilaan
2. **10:30 AM** — Butterfly Sanctuary (nearby)
3. **12:00 PM** — Quisol Mountain View (on the descent)
4. **1:30 PM** — Lunch in Lazi
5. **3:00 PM** — Cambugahay Falls

**Option 2: Complete Island Loop**
Include Mount Bandilaan as part of a full island circumnavigation, hitting the interior when you're between coastal attractions.

## Safety Tips

1. **Go early** — Start your visit by mid-morning at the latest
2. **Check weather** — Don't attempt in rain or immediately after rain
3. **Drive carefully** — The steep, winding road demands attention
4. **Bring a buddy** — Better to travel in pairs, especially if you're not experienced on scooters
5. **Tell someone** — Let your accommodation know your plans
6. **Stay on paths** — The forest can be dense and disorienting

## The Healing Festival Connection

Mount Bandilaan's deepest significance is spiritual. This is where the island's traditional healers come to gather medicinal plants and where they congregate during Holy Week for the Pahi-uli festival.

Even if you don't visit during the festival, you're standing in a place that holds centuries of healing tradition. The plants around you have been used for generations to treat ailments both physical and spiritual. Understanding this context adds depth to what might otherwise seem like just another scenic viewpoint.

## Final Thoughts

Mount Bandilaan isn't on every tourist's radar—and that's part of its charm. While visitors flock to waterfalls and beaches, the island's highest peak offers something different: panoramic views, a connection to Siquijor's healing traditions, and an adventure that takes you beyond the typical coastal experience.

Yes, the road is steep. Yes, it requires some courage if you're not an experienced scooter rider. But the reward—standing at the top of Siquijor, seeing the entire island below you, feeling the cooler mountain air—is absolutely worth the journey.

Just remember: go early, go prepared, and give yourself time to soak in both the view and the unique energy of this sacred place.

You'll know and understand me when you visit this spot. I'm proud of my driving skills after that climb—and you will be too!
